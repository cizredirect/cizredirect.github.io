BELBIN’S SELF-PERCEPTION INVENTORY

This exercise uses a set of questions to determine your natural position(s) within Belbin’s team-role model. Each of us has natural inclinations that cause some activities to be easier and more natural for us, while others require greater effort. Understanding your natural position will allow you to capitalize on those strengths, and better respond to the weaknesses.

Requirements

  • Each of the 7 sections below consists of a general comment followed by a series of related statements. For each section, distribute 10 points among the related statements in a way that best describes your behavior and attitudes. You may assign all the points to one statement if you feel strongly about it, or distribute the points over more than one of the statements if several appeal to you. You are allowed a total of 70 points (10 points for each section).
  • Take your time, think carefully, and respond honestly. Remember that there are no right or wrong answers.
  • After you complete the 7 sections, transcribe your allocation of points into the Points Table on page 6.
  • To interpret your responses in terms of Belbin’s team roles, transcribe the points from the Points Table (page 6) to the Analysis Sheet (page 7). Be careful, as the sequence of statements changes from page 6 to 7.
  • You have 30 minutes to complete the inventory.

BSPI

1. I believe my contribution(s) to a team include:

2. My weakness in teamwork include:

3. When involved in a project with other people:

4. My approach to group work is that:

5. I gain satisfaction in a job because:

6. If I am suddenly given a difficult task with limited time and unfamiliar people:

7. With reference to problems that arise in working in groups:
